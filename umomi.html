UMOMI

UMOMI is a personalized recipe app that allows users to connect with cultures around the world. From recipes around the world to postcards, this app focuses on celebrating diversity.

Overview

The Problem

Cultural identities are often overlooked and communities feel increasingly disconnected even within cooking.

Achievements

This was a submission for You Belong Here, UCI's annual Design-a-thon. Out of the 300+ participants, UMOMI won 'Most Creative Winner' and 'Best Presentation & Story Telling Winner'. Our team made the top 4 finalists!

The Solution

I wanted to create an app where cooking didn't feel transactional but rather personal as if sharing a story with every recipe.

My Role

I led the user interface and user experience design for the project. I focused on branding, wire-framing, and prototyping.

Ch. 1: Design Process

The Spark

The design process began by discovering the story. I spoke with users about their cooking experiences to try to uncover what they thought made recipes personal and meaningful.

Sketch

I turned these into storyboards and userflowers. I wanted to explore concepts like postcards from different cultures and have the storytelling revolve around characters to make UMOMI feel more alive.

Design

I started by designing interfaces that were 'warm' and 'inviting' to make the app feel more like a conversation about culture and diversity rather than just a recipe app.

The Test

We shared our prototypes to college students to see if there any other parts of the 'connecting with culture' that was missing to refine the experience.

Ch. 2: Research

User Interviews

To understand how UMOMI could help users reconnect with their cultural roots, we focused on college students from diverse backgrounds. Our goal was to explore how food and tradition intersect in their daily lives.

We conducted interviews with students aged 18-22 across various universities with each session lasting 10-15 minutes.

Key Questions

  • How connected do you feel to your cultural background?
  • What role does food play in your family or cultural traditions?
  • How often do you try new recipes or cuisines?
  • What would make exploring different cultures through food feel more meaningful and fun?
  • How can an app help you feel more connected to your culture and to others?

Key Insights

Many students shared how hard it was to find culturally meaningful food experiences through existing apps. These insights revealed opportunities for UMOMI to:

  • Make cultural exploration more personal and relatable
  • Create connection and storytelling through recipes
  • Provide a fun, engaging journey for users discovering new cuisines

Ch. 3: The Solution

The Concept

A cultural recipe app that guides you through global ishes and connects you to stories, traditions, and communities behind every meal.

The Userflow

We designed the experience around three core features: discover a recipe, cook with guidance, and unlock the story!

Ch. 4: Final Results

Branding

We crafted a visual identity that was warm and inviting to recreate the feeling of walking into a kitchen filled with the familiar aroma of home-cooked meals.

The color palette leans into soft oranges, greens, and pastel pinks, creating a cozy yet modern interface.

Onboarding

UMOMI begins with a personalized quiz for first users. This is designed to better understand users' needs and preferences.

The quiz includes questions about allergies and dietary restrictions, preferred cuisines, and budget range for meals.

This onboarding flow helps tailer recipe recommendations and ensures accessibility and inclusivity.

Recipe

Each recipe explores new flavors while learning about traditional practices. Our aim is to have users reconnect with their roots or discover new ones through food and storytelling.

Each recipe is paired with a story. The story feature is to bring dishes to life through characters and illustrations. They also come with a personalized "letter" with a short history and cultural context of the dish.
